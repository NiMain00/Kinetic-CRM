# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Internal sales and operations teams in Indonesian enterprises — salespeople, project managers, and procurement staff who manage prospects, projects, approvals, and vendor selection day-to-day. They work in a permission-based environment where different roles see and act on different surfaces.

## Product Purpose

Kinetic CRM manages the full lifecycle from prospect qualification through project delivery, with centralized approval governance at its core. It exists so organizations can maintain control, SLA discipline, and full audit trails across their sales-to-delivery pipeline — reducing the friction of multi-step approvals and keeping every stakeholder visible on what's been decided, by whom, and when.

## Positioning

Approval governance you can trust. Every screen reinforces that this is a system built for accountability — SLA tracking, documented decisions, and an unbroken audit trail from prospect to project completion.

## Brand Personality

Structured · authoritative · clear. Kinetic CRM communicates with the confidence of a system that knows where everything stands. It favors precision over personality, predictability over surprise. Its authority comes from reliability, not from visual weight.

## Anti-references

Neither clunky-enterprise (SAP, Oracle) nor playful-consumer. Avoid the visual heaviness of legacy ERP/CRM interfaces — crowded toolbars, dense data dumps, inconsistent form controls. Also avoid consumer-app aesthetics (rounded everything, playful illustrations, casual typography). Kinetic sits in the middle: professional, purposeful, and unadorned.

## Design Principles

1. **Governance first.** SLA timers, audit trails, and approval chains are the product's reason for being. Every UI decision should make accountability visible and obvious.
2. **Clarity over density.** Teams scan dashboards and approval lists under time pressure. Layout, hierarchy, and typography should surface what needs attention — not bury it in chrome.
3. **Earned trust.** Users need to believe the data. Consistent affordances, predictable navigation, and reliable feedback (saved, submitted, approved, rejected) build that trust interaction by interaction.
4. **Consistent vocabulary.** One button shape, one form control language, one icon system. The same pattern in two places must behave the same way; inconsistency erodes confidence.
5. **Indonesian-market fluency.** Bahasa Indonesia labels, date/number formatting, and workflow terminology that matches how local enterprises operate. The CRM should feel native to its market, not translated.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA across the application. Minimum 4.5:1 contrast for body text (3:1 for large text), keyboard-navigable forms and tables, visible focus indicators, and support for reduced motion preferences.
